## Research Interests

Xi Li's work sits at a rare intersection: **formal logic** (in the tradition of mathematical logic and computability theory) combined with the foundations of **AI and causality**. This is precisely the intersection this repository is building toward.

Core research areas:
1. **Kolmogorov Complexity** — algorithmic information theory as a foundation for intelligence
2. **Solomonoff Induction** — universal prediction from a logical/philosophical perspective
3. **Universal Reinforcement Learning** — AIXI and its logical foundations
4. **Causal Inference** — Pearl's framework and its relationship to formal reasoning
5. **Rational Decision Theory** — FDT, UDT, and the logic of agency
6. **Philosophy of AI** — what does it mean for a machine to understand, reason, or be intelligent?

## Causality Reading Group

Xi Li organizes a **causality reading group** that covers a wide range of topics. The reading list reveals the depth of engagement with this field:

### Core Books Used
1. **Judea Pearl** — *Causality: Models, Reasoning, and Inference* (2009)
2. **Judea Pearl** — *The Book of Why* (2018)
3. **Elias Bareinboim** — *Causal Artificial Intelligence* (draft, 2025)

### Topics Covered in the Reading Group

**Foundations**:
- Causal graphical models and SCMs
- Potential outcomes / Rubin causal model
- do-calculus and identification theory

**Applied Causal Inference**:
- Causal fairness and accountability
- Causal deception (formal definition of dishonesty in causal terms)
- Causality and Large Language Models

**Cutting-Edge Topics**:
- Causal discovery (learning causal structure from data)
- Causal reinforcement learning
- Mental causation (philosophy of mind)
- Backward causation (time-reversed causal claims)
- Quantum causation (violation of classical causal assumptions)

## The Connection to This Repository

Xi Li's teaching and research profile maps almost exactly onto the three pillars of this repository:

| Xi Li's Focus | This Repository Pillar |
|--------------|----------------------|
| Logic (Open Logic Project, computability) | Mathematical Logic & Foundations |
| Universal AI (Hutter's UAI book) | General RL / AIXI |
| Causal Inference (Pearl, Bareinboim) | Causal Inference in RL |

**Uniquely**, Xi Li represents the **philosophical/logical perspective** on these topics — not just the technical one. The Philosophy of AI course using Hutter's UAI book treats AIXI as a theory of what intelligence *is*, not just how to build it.

Key insight from Xi Li's approach: **Kolmogorov complexity + Solomonoff induction + AIXI** form a philosophical theory of rational agency that parallels and extends classical decision theory. This is exactly the bridge between logical foundations and AI that this repository aims to explore.

## Why This Matters

The fact that a logician at a philosophy department is running a reading group on *Causal Artificial Intelligence* using Bareinboim's book signals something important:

**The convergence of logic, causality, and AI is no longer niche.**

Philosophers of mind, logicians, and AI researchers are increasingly speaking the same language — Pearl's Causal Hierarchy, Hutter's AIXI, Garrabrant's Logical Induction. The formal foundations are mature enough to support serious cross-disciplinary engagement.

This repository sits at that exact convergence point.
